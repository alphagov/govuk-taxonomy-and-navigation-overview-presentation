GOV.UK Taxonomy and Navigation

An overview

This presentation is an introduction and overview for the Taxonomy and Navigation parts of GOV.UK.

Taxonomy

Something about classification...

Taxonomy is a unusual work, normally associated with science, especially biology. In general, it's about classification.

Topic Taxonomy

"The Topic Taxonomy is a classification scheme for organising and finding content on GOV.UK, based on its subject area."

GOV.UK has a "Topic Taxonomy". This is both the description, and the proper noun. The key part about the Topic Taxonomy is that it classifies and groups content on GOV.UK by subject area, what the content is about. This excludes grouping content by entities, e.g. People, Oranisations, Countries, Languages. Even if you say this content is "about" a person, having a taxon representing a person would not fit within the Topic Taxonomy. This is not to say that recording association with entities is not useful, it normally is. Just that it should be done through other means.

Topic Taxonomy Structure

  • It's a (mathematical) tree
  • The root is the homepage
The Topic Taxonomy is a tree, in the mathematical sense. The root is the homepage, and the nodes are content items with a `document_type` of `taxon`. Links in the Publishing API are used for the edges in the tree, in total 4 link types are used, two are explicit, and two are implicit.

Aboutness and Specificity

  • The Topic Taxonomy is about the subject area of content, or "aboutness"
  • Taxons lower down the tree should be more specific
As you go down the tree, the subject area covered by the individual taxons should get smaller, and more specific.

Content is "tagged" to taxons

This is a visualisation of the volume of content tagged to each taxon.

Content on GOV.UK can be tagged to one or more taxons. This association is also represented by a link in the Publishing API with the `link_type` taxons. This is a visualisation in Content Tagger showing the volume of content tagged to these taxons.

Topic Taxonomy Scope

All content items, except the ones that aren't content...

  • taxon
  • person
  • gone
  • ...

For the full list, see document_types_excluded_from_the_topic_taxonomy.yml

For measuring the amount of content tagged to the Topic Taxonomy, we need a way of distinguishing the content on GOV.UK which is actually content, from the Content which isn't. To do this, there is a list of `document_type`s which are considered out of scope. For example, `taxon` is out of scope as the content items of `document_type` taxon represent nodes in the Topic Taxnomy. Content items with a `document_type` of gone are used to represent content which has been unpublished, so these are excluded.
With this list of excluded document types, you can work out how much content (excluding the out of scope content) is present on GOV.UK, and how much of that is tagged to the Topic Taxonomy. This is monitored and recorded in Graphite, and visible on this Grafana dashboard.

Uses of the Topic Taxonomy

  • Topic pages
  • Email subscriptions
  • ...
The Topic Taxonomy should be thought about as a source of information. That information can be used in various ways, for example, navigation pages, email subscriptions, and maybe other things.

Work on navigation pages powered by the Topic Taxonomy began in the Finding Things team, and the "beta" happened back in March 2017. In March this year, around Quarter 4, the new design for the Topic Taxonomy powered navigation pages, now called Topic Pages was deployed. The big change in this 2nd iteration is that more content is accessible from the pages, previously it was limited to guidance content.
